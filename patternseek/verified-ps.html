A verification mark for the semantic economy ahead

VerifiedPS is a lightweight interface layer that lets clients, vendors, partners, and individual participants submit an artifact (claim, dataset, document, KPI definition, brand narrative, research note) and receive a CDI-aligned record + provenance hooks that can be re-run across multiple model contexts.

How it works (v0 public preview)

1) Register an artifact

A publication, internal memo, dataset, product claim, survey instrument, KPI spec, or research thread. You choose what’s public vs private; the public site can remain “metadata-only” until connected to the private repo.

2) Bind to CDI terms

Key terms map to the Codified Definitions Index so “meaning” is not vibes-based. Changes become diffs with effective dates, status, linked assets, and provenance.

3) Run calibration passes

Cross-model checks for semantic drift, tone reinforcement, and evidence-weighted agreement (SRA). Output: an auditable trail showing what changed, why, and under which constraints.

4) Issue VerifiedPS record

A stable reference handle you can re-run through studios/models later (or on a schedule), without exposing private payloads — only the permissioned aggregate and trace.

Who uses it

Individuals

Contribute insights non-intrusively and earn tokenized participation credit (future), without being forced into someone else’s platform preferences.

Enterprises & institutions

Register internal intelligence (research, KPIs, policies, product claims) into a calibration loop that improves coherence across teams and AI deployments.

Vendors & data partners

Offer “VerifiedPS-ready” assets that preserve nuance, provenance, and definitional stability across the LLM ecosystem— without disclosing proprietary methods.
