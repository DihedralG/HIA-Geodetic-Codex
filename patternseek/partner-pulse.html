What Partner Pulse is

Partner Pulse is a pilot framework for institutions, sponsors, studios, and research teams to engage opt-in participants through a lightweight daily “Calibration Pulse.” It produces primary research signals that are consent-driven, replayable, and bias-aware — without turning participants into targets.

  • Opt-in by design — participants choose what to share, and what persists.
  • Calibration, not enforcement — no “truth score,” no ranking, no gatekeeping.
  • Provenance-ready — outputs can be tied to CDI diffs and traceable assets.

Why partners use it

Better primary research

Replace passive analytics with real signals: what people found meaningful, what changed their view, and what they want to explore next — captured with consent.

Outcome: insight you can defend, not just clicks you can count.

Bias-aware testing

Prompt design can be moderated to reduce leading questions, push-polling, and response conditioning — while preserving disagreement and uncertainty.

Outcome: cleaner signal, less manipulation risk.

Replayable calibration

Run the same pulse loop across different cohorts or time windows and compare drift, variance, and boundary stability — without turning people into a scoreboard.

Outcome: continuity you can measure over time.

What participants do

Participants complete a short, optional check-in (~30 seconds). They select touchpoints like Information, Work, Learning, Recreation, and Discovery — then optionally choose a pathway: Share Insight, Discover Something New, or Submit Pulse.

Share Insight

A short reflection — what mattered, what changed, what felt true, or what felt off. This is where nuance lives.

Discover Something New

Participant-led discovery loops: curated prompts, sample sets, and sponsor-supported “tries” that feel like offers — not targeting.

Submit Pulse

A low-friction option: contribute a signal without writing. Participation remains lightweight.

Important: PatternSeek doesn’t watch people. It listens when they choose to share — and makes it worth the time.

No ranking people

No forced consensus
