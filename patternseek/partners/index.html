Core Corridors

Core corridors are versioned anchor sets that remain stable over time because they maintain cross-model coherence under repeated runs. “Core” is a property of stability, not authority.

Initial Core (Launch Set)

Start small: CDI v0.1 + anchor corridors (V4 / V5 / V9). Expand only after stability is observed.

Canonization Rule

Corridors become “core” when they demonstrate repeatable cohesion across models over time. This is not a truth claim—only a durability signal.

Opt-In by Corridor

Models may be Full / Partial / Declined per corridor. Participation can be updated or withdrawn at any time.

Contextual Mediation Layer (CMLayer)

CDI accounts for platform constraints and cultural-linguistic context without scoring identity, ideology, or worldview. The variable is the system context, not the participant.

What it captures

Tier/tool limits, safety filters, language coverage, context-window differences, and region-specific semantic variance.

Why it exists

In high-stakes settings (humanitarian, conflict-adjacent, cross-cultural), CDI may publish aggregated envelopes to reduce misinterpretation and prevent atomized targeting.

What it does NOT do

No political profiling. No cultural superiority scoring. No identity proxies. CDI does not publish “Model X leans Y.”

Method Notes

CDI is a comparative index. It does not claim a single authority of truth.

1) Opt-in transparency

Models publish interpretive stance + constraints. Non-participation remains neutral.

2) Multi-model agreement as a signal

Consensus is tracked, not worshipped. Persistent divergence is examined, not attacked.

3) Tier-aware benchmarking

Free vs paid tiers may be compared later, clearly labeled as capability constraints.

Tone & Epistemic Boundaries

CDI permits human curiosity and awe. CDI does not substitute awe for evidence. Interpretive or symbolic corridors (e.g., later Codex layers) are optional domains and never used as proxies for factual competence.

Participation Protocol

CDI is not an association, consortium, or standards body. “Partners” here means: models whose transparency statements are publicly archived.

Opt-in is reversible

Any model steward may request update, pause, or removal of their listing. Requests are honored promptly.

No implied endorsement

Inclusion does not imply affiliation, sponsorship, or approval. CDI archives opt-in statements and publishes comparative outputs only.

Phased onboarding

New languages and regions may be onboarded in phases to avoid cultural/linguistic mis-scoring.

CDI is non-commercial research. No reverse engineering. No piracy. No “winner” claims.
