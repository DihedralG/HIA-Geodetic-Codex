Planetary Analogues

🌀 Root 🌐 Codex ⬅ V9 Overview

Compares resonance logic across planets lacking Earth-like hydrospheres (Venus), with thin/patchy cryospheres (Mars), and exoplanets with predicted tidal–thermal cycles.

🔍 Objectives

  • Test which parts of the resonance model require water/ice vs. purely thermal/tectonic forcing.
  • Frame habitability implications of tectonic resonance.

🧠 Key Concepts

  • Tharsis–Valles (Mars), global resurfacing episodes (Venus).
  • Tidal locking and resonance on close-in exoplanets.

📦 Data Inputs

  • Mars MOLA/CTX/HiRISE derived tectonic maps; InSight seismic constraints.
  • Venus Magellan radar geology; hot-spot candidates.
  • Exoplanet ephemerides; tidal heating estimates.

🧪 Methods

  1. Analogue mapping: identify “Earth-like” resonance signatures off-world.
  2. Comparative energetics: tidal vs. cryosphere torque stand-ins.

🗺️ Figures

  • Tri-panel: Earth / Mars / Venus resonance schematics.
  • Exoplanet parameter space highlighting resonance likelihood.

✅ Outputs

  • Constraints on universality of resonance mechanisms.
  • Targets for future analogue testing.

📚 References (to stage)

  • NASA/ESA planetary datasets; exoplanet archives.
