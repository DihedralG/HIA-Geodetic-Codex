Orbital Forcing & Cryosphere Feedbacks

🌀 Root 🌐 Codex ⬅ V9 Overview

This module maps Milankovitch pacing (precession, obliquity, eccentricity) to cryosphere growth/retreat, and sets up the analysis for meltwater pulses that prime rift activation and anomaly pairings.

🔍 Objectives

  • Quantify relationships between orbital cycles and ice-volume proxies.
  • Characterize lead–lag structures between orbital forcing and cryosphere response.
  • Identify pulse sequences that correlate with rift initiation and uplift counterweights.

🧠 Key Concepts

  • Precession (≈19–23 kyr) – seasonal/hemispheric insolation swings; often drives monsoon–ice feedbacks.
  • Obliquity (≈41 kyr) – high-latitude summer insolation; strong control on ice-sheet mass balance.
  • Eccentricity (≈100/405 kyr) – amplitude modulator for precession; tied to late-Pleistocene pacing.
  • Amplifiers – albedo, ocean heat transport, and freshwater routing that sharpen deglaciation pulses.

  • Deglacial events: MWP-1A, MWP-1B, Younger Dryas termination markers.

🧪 Methods (analysis plan)

  1. Spectral diagnostics: multitaper/FFT on ice proxies; verify 23/41/100/405 kyr bands.
  2. Cross-correlation: orbital indices vs. ice proxies to estimate lead–lag (regional & global).
  3. Event alignment: align meltwater pulses to orbital phase; annotate cap dominance (N vs S).
  4. Coupling to rifts: join pulse windows to Codex rift entries; test for clustering near anomaly pairings.
  5. Robustness: bootstrap confidence intervals; shuffle tests against null (phase-scrambled indices).
